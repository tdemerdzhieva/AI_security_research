This week's lesson: sometimes the most dangerous attacks are also the most boring ones.

Label flipping doesn't touch the data. No pixels moved, no features shifted. You just swap the answers the model is supposed to learn from.

I implemented two versions.

The basic one flips 60% of labels randomly. The model drops from 99% accuracy to near zero. Simple, loud, obvious if anyone is paying attention.

The targeted one is different. You only flip labels for one class out of four. Half go to the neighbor below, half to the neighbor above. Everything else stays perfect. Overall accuracy barely moves.

That second one kept me thinking for a while. Most teams monitor overall accuracy. Nobody wakes up at 3am to check per-class recall. So the model ships. It works great on three out of four categories. And silently fails on the one that matters.

Fraud detection that misses one fraud type. Medical imaging that struggles with one specific finding. Intrusion detection that ignores one attack pattern.

The boring attack. The one that looks like a data quality problem until it isn't.
